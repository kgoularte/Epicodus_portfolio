What Brought Me to Epicodus

Programming bootcamps were something that I have known about for a year or two but didn't orginally look into. I went to an alternative highschool that was set up as a go at your own pace structure that allowed me to graduate a year and a half early. It wasn't uncommon at my school to graduate a semester early or even a year early but a year and a half was actually a new record me and my good friend set. I went on to attend the local college in my hometown the following fall. I started out taking more than full time class load and was doing very well. over the next two years this started to change. I started to really have issues with my school and realized that I didn't do well with the traditional way of learning, like most people, and I was ready to try something new.

I had heard about code schools awhile back but a lot of the time they have a pretty bad stigma around them. when going the untraditional route there is almost always a higher risk of failure. But I really didn't want to wait three more years to get a degree so I started looking into programming bootcamps and found Epicodus. after some time I decide to come to a workshop and got the chance to see what a good program it is and two months later here I am! I am not actually from Portland and I moved to Vancouver the weekend before class started. Not only is everything at epicodus new to me but really everything in my life is right now.
